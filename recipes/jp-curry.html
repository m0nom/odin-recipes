Japanese curry

ingredients

We need ingredients yes

  • 2 Carrots
  • 2 Onion
  • 3 Potatoes
  • 1 Tsp Ginger (Grated)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 230g Chicken thighs
  • Ground black pepper
  • 4 Tbsp butter
  • 4 Cups chicken stock (To make it less sodium-y add half stock half water)
  • 1 Tbsp Honey
  • 1 Tbsp Soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp ketchup
  • 1 pack japanese curry roux
  • Cooked rice (day prior)

Prep Work

  1. ACut the 2 onions into wedges. Depending on how much you like the taste and feel of onion youâ€™ll make them bigger or smaller.
  2. Peel 2 carrots into rolling wedges.
  3. (Peel) and cut the 3 potatoes into quarters, soak them in water for 15 minutes to remove excess starch.
  4. Grate the ginger and mince the garlic and set aside
  5. Remove the extra fat from the chicken, and cut into bite-sized pieces. If the roux is not that salty, lightly season the chicken with kosher salt.

Directions

  1. Heat the butter over high heat until lightly smoking. Reduce heat to medium-high. Add the chicken and cook, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned, about 3 minutes. Add the onions, season with salt and pepper, and cook, stirring, until the onions are softened, about 3 minutes.
  2. Add the garlic and ginger and cook, stirring, until aromatic, about 30 seconds. Add the curry powder and cook, stirring, until lightly toasted, about 30 seconds.
  3. Add the flour and cook, stirring, until no pockets of dry flour remain, about 30 seconds. Add the broth or water and stir to dissolve the flour. Add the potatoes, carrots, honey, and raisins. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to maintain a bare simmer. Cover and cook until the potatoes and carrots are tender but not falling apart, about 10 minutes.
